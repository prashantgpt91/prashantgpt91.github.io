The problem

Enterprises are building AI like it's still 2023

One application, one foundation model, everything routed through it. That pattern breaks the moment AI becomes real infrastructure.

Application → Single Foundation Model → Everything

Rising costs

Every request — trivial or complex — runs through the most expensive model available.

Vendor lock-in

Mission-critical workflows are welded to a single provider's roadmap, pricing, and outages.

Compliance risk

Data residency, audit, and governance get harder with every new region and workload.

Performance bottlenecks

Simple tasks sit behind oversized infrastructure, paying a latency tax for no reason.

No visibility

Teams can't see where AI spend goes, which workflows drive it, or what's worth optimizing.

Operational drag

Each new model, vendor, and deployment adds another system for someone to babysit.

The solution

A control plane for AI workloads

Five capabilities in one layer that sits between your applications and every engine they call.

Routing

Workload Router

Decomposes each request and sends every step to the optimal execution engine — by cost, latency, accuracy, and policy. The intelligence that turns a pile of models into a system.

Policy

Policy Engine

Declarative rules for where workloads may run — by region, vendor, data class, or team.

Governance

Governance Layer

Centralized security, RBAC, and audit trails across every model and every call.

Cost

Cost Optimizer

Continuously shifts work to the cheapest engine that meets the bar — and proves the savings.

Observability

Observability Platform

Live visibility into cost, latency, routing decisions, and performance — per team and workflow.

Live routing engine

Type any workload. Watch the router pick a model per step.

Fabric decomposes the request, routes each sub-task to the smallest capable engine — a fine-tuned SLM or specialist — and escalates only the genuinely hard step to a frontier model. Cost and latency are computed live against an all-frontier baseline.
